Working papers
  • Effects of Limited Traffic Zones on the rental housing market: evidence from Milan [Job Market Paper] Adelia Fatikhova (Job Market paper).

    Traffic-calming policies have become a popular measure to improve the urban environment with respect to air quality, congestion, traffic, etc. Such policies often come with overlooked unintended effects, which are crucial to understand in order to achieve green policy objectives. In this paper, I focus on a recent driving policy shift, introduced in Milan, and explore its impacts on the rental housing market. By gathering daily housing data, published on the Italian real estate website Immobiliare throughout 2022, and employing difference-in-difference methodology with various fixed effects, I find that the proximity to the metro capitalizes in a 5%-7% price increase. Additionally, I employ triple difference-in-differences to understand the capitalization of other means of public transport into rental prices. [Slides]

  • Regional green-tech diversification and inter-regional green knowledge flows: EU evidence. Adelia Fatikhova, Sandro Montresor (UNITN) and Fabrizio Fusillo (UNITO). (Under review in Economic Geography)

    This work addresses the role of inter-regional linkages in green economic transition among European regions. We exploit the newly published EUREGIO database to build an indicator of regional participation in the (European) network of embodied inter-regional green knowledge flows and evaluate its role in diversifying into green technologies. We build a matrix of patent-to-industry probability weights, which allows us to grasp those industries that are contributing to the green transition the most and apply social network analysis to measure regional participation. Our results suggest that the regional exposure to green knowledge generated and exchanged out of the regional (and national) boundaries has a crucial role in fostering the regional capacity to master new environmental technologies over time.
